Pronunciations






Note: syllables in Turkish tend to end after a vowel sound, and consonants usually dont lead from the previous vowel in a different syllable(in other words, they're pronounced normally).
Phonotactics, Syllable Structure, etc are not mentioned in this section.
If random capitalized symbols that do not appear in Turkish appear, reload the page.

















Terms

Roundness - A vowel sound is rounded if you round your lips to produce it

Syllable-Initial - Describes something that is in the first syllable

Syllable-Final - Describes something that is in the last syllable

Turkish pronounciations are always consistent excluding foreign and loan words.

"â" is just an ah sound but palatalizes the letter before it. This circumflex a is slowly becoming obsolete, but if you see it(and can't palatalize consonants), pretend to see "ia(ee-ah)" instead of "â" and pronounce both the I and the A.   1. /k/ and /g/ when next to a front vowel(/e, i, œ, y/) are palatalized to /c/ and /ɟ/.

  2. ğ is usually silent, it lengthens the vowel sound preceding it if it is before a consonant or syllable-final. Between front vowels it becomes /j/. Otherwise, it is entirely silent and does nothing.

  3. l is pronounced as /l/ next to front vowels, and /ɫ/ next to other vowels.

  4. r at the end of words sounds like it has an extra "sh" sound that is pretty quiet(ex. bir - beersh)
