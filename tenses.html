Tenses

Tenses are relatively simple, however they span across multiple moods. Moods will be mentioned later on, but the moods do effect how some tenses work, for now we will use the simplest mood(called Indictative) which doesn't add any extra markers. In Turkish there are 4 main tenses: Aorist, Imperfective, Perfective, and Future tense(Negative Aorist also exists, but can be considered just Aorist with special rules for negation). Keep in mind that tenses can mix with each other(imperfective + perfective = past tense continous(ex. I was eating)).

  1. Aorist - -²r/-⁴r

  2. Imperfective - -⁴yor

  3. Perfective - -t/d⁴

  4. Future - -(y)²c²k/ğ

Below is a table that orders the information above along with the conjugations(the verb "yemek" will be used as an example):

Present Past Future
Aorist Imperfective Imperfective Perfective
Ben (I) Yerim Yiyorum Yedim Yiyordum Yiyeceğim
Sen (You) Yersin Yiyorsun Yedin Yiyordun Yiyeceksin
O (He/She/It) Yer Yiyor Yedi Yiyordu Yiyecek
Biz (We) Yeriz Yiyoruz Yedik Yiyorduk Yiyeceğiz
Siz (You(plural)) Yersiniz Yiyorsunuz Yediniz Yiyordunuz Yiyeceksiniz
Onlar (They) Yerler Yiyorlar Yediler Yiyorlardı Yiyecekler
